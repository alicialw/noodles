NOODLES & BEYOND

The affective data behind comfort food

In a world obsessed with calories and nutrients, we've overlooked what truly nourishes us.

For generations, comfort foods like instant noodles have been simultaneously craved and shamed — guilty pleasures that remain universal in our kitchens.

Through our cultural probe following 18 university students, we've uncovered the invisible emotional data behind our comfort food rituals, revealing patterns of emotional nourishment that conventional nutrition labels never capture.

Scroll to discover the emotional nutrition hiding in your bowl.

SELF-CARE: THE ART OF PERSONAL ALCHEMY

We instinctively modify our instant noodles with additional ingredients. These modifications aren't just about taste — they're emotional self-care rituals disguised as cooking.

An egg added after a tough day.

A handful of greens when we need to feel virtuous.

Extra chili during creative blocks.

These aren't random additions — they're intuitive emotional prescriptions.

MULTITASKING: BEYOND JUST EATING

Watch how our bowls move around our spaces — desk corners during study sessions, coffee tables during movie nights, kitchen counters during late-night talks.

Our cultural probe revealed these aren't just meals — they're emotional companions to our activities. The bowl of noodles becomes a steady presence through everything from heartbreaks to celebrations, deadlines to discoveries.

Sometimes it's not about the noodles at all, but the gentle rhythm they bring to chaotic moments.

MEMORY: THE TASTE OF NOSTALGIA

Ever notice how a specific flavor can instantly transport you?

Every single word in our memory vessels — warm, midnight, laughter, lonely, home — becomes threads connecting us to entire chapters of our lives. These emotional keywords weave together across shared themes: family gatherings, late-night study sessions, first apartments where familiar aromas created instant belonging.

The same store-bought packet somehow becomes uniquely ours through repetition and context, storing emotional data like rings in a tree—nourishing something deeper than just our bodies.

ABOUT THE PROJECT

This project doesn't judge our comfort food choices but invites us to see the emotional nourishment they provide.

The data vessels make visible what we've always felt but rarely discussed: food feeds more than just our bodies.
